Homeless Animals in The Bahamas

Homeless/Stray Animals

Homeless (or stray) animals are animals that have been abandoned and neglected. They are either,
given up by their owners, or found by animal control, rescued and put into shelters. However, there
are few animal shelters in The Bahamas, and many stray animals are still wandering about the
streets in the islands of The Bahamas. While there are many national parks in The Bahamas, these
mostly cater to threatened and endangered species. Therefore, not much effort is put into finding,
rescuing and rehabilitating more common animals such as cats and dogs.

How Many are There?

When it comes to dogs and cats, Nassau is much like other cities, hundreds of strays on the streets and disease occurs. Homeless animals outnumber homeless people 5 to 1. According to The Bahamas
Advocates for Animal Rights group, there are 2,000 unowned dogs and hundreds of stray cats in New Providence. However, after Hurricane Dorian, these numbers increased greatly as many homes were destroyed and the people who these animals depended on tragically died. Now there are more
stray/homeless animals than ever before.









What is Being Done to Alleviate this Problem?

As mentioned before, there are organizations in The Bahamas, such as The Bahamas National Trust,
