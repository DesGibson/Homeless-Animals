What Problem Am I Trying To Solve?

As a resident in The Bahamas, and as someone who grew up in New Providence
(Nassau), I have noticed a significant amount of animals roaming the streets
(mainly cats and dogs). These animals pose a threat, not only to people who reside
in the Bahams, but also to themselves. Homeless animals in The Bahamas are
considered to be 'feral' or 'wild'. They often attack and chase people (or each other)
since there is no leash stopping them. Even animals that are on a leash find a way
to injure/hurt innocent bystanders. According to statistics, forty-five percent of
tourists report seeing roaming dogs, and two percent state they were "scared" by
their presence. This problem became worse when the total number of stray animals
in The Bahamas increased in 2019 after Hurricane Dorian destroyed many homes
in Grand Bahama and Abaco.









How Do I Plan To Solve This Problem?

To help local organizations, such as The Bahamas National Trust and
The Bahamas Humane Society with controlling the stray, endangered
and threatened animal populations in The Bahamas, I decided to open
an animal shelter, to take care of stray animals, a pet store, to sell pet
food, toys, clothes, etc., and a pet adoption centre, to find a new home
for the stray animals.

Where Will the Proceeds Go?

  1. Back to the Cause

    2. The aim of D's Anaimal Shelter is to make the islands of The Bahamas
    a safer place, for those who reside there, by getting homeless animals
    off the streets and finding them a proper home. So, the first portion of
    the proceeds, made by the organization, will go towards the cause for
    which it exists, to decrease the stray animal population in The Bahamas.





  2. Running The Business

    3. Every business organization (including non-profit organizations) requires funds
    to operate. Therefore, the second portion of the money, that D's Animal Shelter
    makes, will go towards paying for the things which are neccessary to keep the
    business running (includes water, electricity, employees, research, marketing
    and other expenses).





  3. Helping Other Animal Shelters and Organizations

    4. D's Animal Shelter is not the only Bahamian organization that is dedicated
    to the protection, preservation and care of animals. There are other non-profit
    organizations in The Bahamas that exist for the same purpose and rely on
    donations to operate. So, the third portion of the proceeds will be donated to
    these organizations to allow them to continue the work they are doing and to
    help them plan future fundraisers.
